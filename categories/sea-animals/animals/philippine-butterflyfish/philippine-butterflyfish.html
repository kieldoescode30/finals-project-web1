“Philippine Butterflyfish”

General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Butterflyfish

Scientific Name:

Chaetodon adiergastos

Type:

Marine Fish

Trophic Categorization:

Philippine butterflyfish (family Chaetodontidae) are generally considered omnivores. These fish predominantly feed on coral polyps, small invertebrates, and algae, making them omnivorous with a strong preference for carnivorous diet components. By preying on these organisms, butterflyfish help control coral and invertebrate populations, playing a vital role in maintaining the balance and health of coral reef ecosystems.

Place of Origin:

The Philippine Butterflyfish is native to the tropical waters of the Western Pacific Ocean, particularly found in the Philippines and surrounding areas.

Sound of Animal:

Unknown

Description & Characteristics

The Philippine Butterflyfish typically has a disc-shaped body with rounded fins. It is adorned with vibrant colors and intricate patterns, including stripes and spots, which help it blend into its coral reef habitat. The body coloration can range from yellow to white with black markings. It has a distinctive dark band running vertically through its eye.
