General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Sea Urchin

Scientific Name:

Echinoidea

Type:

Echinoderms

Trophic Categorization:

Sea urchins are primarily categorized as herbivores in the trophic hierarchy of marine ecosystems. As herbivores, their primary diet consists of algae, seaweed, and other plant material. They use their specialized mouthparts, known as Aristotle's lantern, to scrape algae off rocks and other surfaces. By consuming algae, sea urchins play a crucial role in controlling algal growth and maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems.

Place of Origin:

Sea urchins are found in oceans worldwide, inhabiting a variety of marine environments from shallow coastal waters to the deep sea. As such, they do not have a single specific place of origin. Instead, they have evolved and diversified over millions of years in different oceanic regions around the globe. This widespread distribution has led to the existence of numerous species adapted to various environmental conditions, ranging from tropical coral reefs to polar regions.

Sound of Animal:

Unknown

Description & Characteristics

Sea urchins are marine echinoderms characterized by their globular or flattened bodies covered in movable spines, serving various functions including defense and locomotion. With a specialized feeding structure known as Aristotle's lantern, they primarily graze on algae and other plant material, playing a crucial role in controlling algal growth and maintaining the health of marine habitats such as coral reefs and rocky shores.
