Scientific Name:

Type:

Bird

Trophic Categorization:

The Palawan peacock-pheasant (Polyplectron napoleonis) is primarily an omnivore, fitting into the trophic category of omnivores. This means it consumes a variety of food sources, including both plant and animal matter.

Place of Origin:

The Palawan peacock-pheasant (Polyplectron napoleonis) is endemic to the island of Palawan in the Philippines. The Palawan peacock-pheasant (Polyplectron napoleonis) is endemic to the island of Palawan in the Philippines

Sound of Animal:

This vulnerable species communicates through a variety of vocalizations, including high-pitched whistles, cackles, clucks, and hooting sounds, which are used for mating, territorial displays, and signaling danger.

Description & Characteristics

For the male's iridescent blue-green plumage, vibrant blue facial skin, and eye-spotted tail feathers used in elaborate courtship displays. Females are smaller, with more subdued brown plumage for camouflage.
