“Philippine Warty Pig”

/ ˈwɔːrti /

Return Trail

General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Warty Pig

Scientific Name:

Sus philippensis

Type:

Mammal

Trophic Categorization:

Philippine Warty Pig is that of an omnivore. These pigs are known to have an omnivorous diet, consuming a variety of foods including leaves, grass, roots, fruits, flowers, insects, and other animal matter. Their diet consists of both plant-based materials and animal sources, which aligns with the characteristics of an omnivorous feeding behavior.

Place of Origin:

Endemic to the Philippines, specifically found on various islands including Luzon, Polillo, Catanduanes, Mindoro, Samar, Leyte, Mindanao, Jolo, and possibly other islands.

Sound of Animal:

A series of grunts, squeals, and similar vocalizations.

Description & Characteristics

Philippine Warty Pig is a robustly built species with a coarse, bristly, blackish coat that features a scattering of silvery white hairs on the sides. These pigs have tufts of hair on the top of their head and on the lower sides of their jaws, as well as four warts on their faces.
