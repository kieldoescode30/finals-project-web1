General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Tarsier

Scientific Name:

Tarsius syrichta

Type:

Mammal

Trophic Categorization:

Primarily insectivorous, meaning it feeds on insects, spiders, small crustaceans, and small vertebrates such as lizards and birds.

Place of Origin:

In the southeastern Philippines, specifically native to the islands of Leyte, Samar, Dinagat, Siargao, Bohol, Mindanao, Basilan, and Maripipi.

Sound of Animal:

Described as a high-pitched squeak.

Description & Characteristics

Small size, Fur coloration, has a narrow, bald tail, elongated hindlimbs and short forelimbs, has disproportionately large eyes, the largest eye-to-body weight ratio of all mammals, has large, mobile ears that are constantly moving to detect any movement, tarsier's tail and feet are largely hairless, which helps distinguish it from other tarsier species, and There is no notable physical difference between male and female Philippine tarsiers.
