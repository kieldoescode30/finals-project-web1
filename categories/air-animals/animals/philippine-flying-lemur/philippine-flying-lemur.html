General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Flying lemur

Scientific Name:

Colugo

Type:

Herbivorous

Trophic Categorization:

The trophic classification or trophic categorization of the Philippine Flying Lemur (Colugo) can be determined based on its diet and ecological role within its habitat. The provided sources describe the Philippine Flying Lemur as herbivorous, feeding on leaves, fruit, sap, shoots, and flowers.This dietary information indicates that the Philippine Flying Lemur primarily consumes plant-based foods, placing it in the herbivore trophic category. As an herbivore, the Philippine Flying Lemur plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by contributing to seed dispersal and vegetation dynamics through its feeding habits.

Place of Origin:

The Philippine Flying Lemur (Colugo) is endemic to the southern Philippines, with its population concentrated in the Mindanao region and Bohol. It may also be found in Samar and Leyte.

Sound of Animal:

The Philippine flying lemur is generally quiet. It primarily relies on its highly developed senses of sight, touch, and smell for communication and navigation.

Description & Characteristics

Colugo, also known as Philippine Flying Lemur, has a wide head and rostrum with a robust mandible for increased bite strength, small ears, and big eyes with unique photoreceptor adaptations.Its clawed feet are large and sharp with an incredible grip strength.
