Air Animal:

“Philippine Falconet”

/ ˌfal-kə-ˈnet /

Return Trail

General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Falconet

Scientific Name:

Microhierax erythrogenys

Type:

Carnivore

Trophic Categorization:

Classification of the Philippine Falconet (Microhierax erythrogenys) is that of an insectivore.This bird of prey primarily hunts flying insects near open forest edges, perching on prominent branches or the canopy of old dead trees to catch its prey.

Place of Origin:

The Philippine Falconet is endemic to the Philippine archipelago, found on islands such as Luzon, Mindoro, Negros, Catanduanes, Bohol, Calicoan, Samar, Leyte, Cebu, Mindanao, and Panay.It inhabits subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests, often seen near open forest edges.

Sound of Animal:

Described as a descending, mouse-like squeak, "wiiuuu!"

Description & Characteristics

The Philippine Falconet is a very small bird of prey, measuring 15-18 cm in length with a wingspan of 32-37 cm and a body mass of 37-52 g.It has a distinctive black and white plumage coloration. The upperparts from head to tail are uniformly black, while the underparts from the cheek to the lower neck are white, with a washed buff belly.The underwing coverts are black, and the flight feathers are inconspicuously barred white.Females are slightly larger, up to 20% bigger than males, and have all-black underwings, unlike the males which have white spots on the inner webs of the remiges.The southern subspecies are slightly larger than the northern subspecies, indicating some degree of geographic variation.
