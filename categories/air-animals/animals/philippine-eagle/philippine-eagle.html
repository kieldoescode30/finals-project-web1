General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Eagle

Scientific Name:

Pithecophaga jefferyi

Type:

Carnivore

Trophic Categorization:

The Philippine eagle is a raptor or bird of prey that feeds primarily on a variety of animal prey, including monkeys, birds, flying foxes, giant cloud-rats, Asian palm civets, flying squirrels, tree squirrels, fruit bats, reptiles (large snakes and lizards), and even other birds of prey.

Place of Origin:

The Philippine eagle is found only in the Philippines, specifically on four major islands: Luzon, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao.

Sound of Animal:

Philippine Eagle is a descending, whistled "weeuuuu!" repeated at intervals.

Description & Characteristics

Large size,considered the largest extant eagle in the world, measuring 86-102 cm (2.82-3.35 ft) in length, has brown and white-colored plumage.has a shaggy, mane-like crest of long, brown feathers on its nape, Size and Measurements: average length is 95 cm (3.1 ft) for males and 105 cm (3.4 ft) for females, The wingspan can extend up to 7 feet, Females are typically about 10% large than males on average.
