General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Hawk-owl

Scientific Name:

Ninox philippensis

Type:

Carnivore

Trophic Categorization:

Philippine Hawk-Owl is classified as a carnivore, meaning it is a predatory bird that feeds primarily on small animals and other prey.

Place of Origin:

The Philippine Hawk-Owl (Ninox philippensis) is endemic to the Philippines, with various species found across different islands in the Philippine archipelago.

Sound of Animal:

Philippine Hawk-owl is known for its distinctive calls, which are often described as growling or hooting sounds. These calls are typically heard at night and are an important part of the bird's communication and mating behaviors.

Description & Characteristics

The Philippine Hawk-Owl is a species complex, consisting of 7 known species found across different islands in the Philippines.They are earless owls, meaning they lack the prominent ear tufts seen in some other owl species.The males and females of the different Philippine Hawk-Owl species look very similar, with only minor differences in size and patterning on the underside.The species range in size from 21.0 to 26.5 cm in total length, including the tail, and have wingspans of 16.5 to 20.5 cm.The plumage patterns vary, with some species having a checked pattern on the underside, while others have a striped pattern.The Luzon, Mindoro, Sulu, Mindanao, Romblon, Cebu, and Camiguin Hawk-Owls are the 7 recognized species within this complex.
