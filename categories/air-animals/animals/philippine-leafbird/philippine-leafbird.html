General Information

This section offers a comprehensive overview of the Philippine Leafbird

Scientific Name:

Chloropsis flavipennis

Type:

Omnivore

Trophic Categorization:

Philippine Leafbird being classified as an omnivore, consuming both plant and animal food sources within its tropical forest ecosystem. The lack of a more specific trophic categorization in the sources suggests an omnivorous classification is the most appropriate.

Place of Origin:

Philippine leafbird is a species of bird in the family Chloropidae. It is endemic to the Philippines. It is found in the islands of Mindanao, Leyte, and Cebu.

Sound of Animal:

Simple, medium-pitched whistled phrases, A series of repeated "tyup!" notes.

Description & Characteristics

Philippine Leafbird is a vibrant lime-green bird found in the Philippines, known for its colorful plumage, insectivorous diet, and adaptations for nectar feeding. It plays a crucial role in pollination and exists in tropical forest habitats, facing threats from habitat loss and capture for the pet trade.
